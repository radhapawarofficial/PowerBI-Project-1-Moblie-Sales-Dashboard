🎯 Project Overview

This dashboard provides a comprehensive view of mobile sales data for top smartphone brands across Indian cities. It enables business stakeholders to track revenue performance, customer satisfaction, transaction trends, and payment behavior — all through interactive filtering by month, brand, and payment method.

📊 Dashboard Features

KPI Cards — Total Sales, Transactions, Average Value, and Quantity at a glance
India Map Visual — City-wise total sales plotted geographically across 15+ Indian cities including Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Lucknow, Kolkata, and more
Brand Performance Table — Total sales breakdown by Apple, OnePlus, Samsung, Vivo, and Xiaomi
Top 3 Models — Bar chart comparing best-selling mobile models (iPhone SE, OnePlus Nord, Galaxy Note 20)
Customer Ratings Distribution — Horizontal bar chart showing rating spread (1–5 stars)
Payment Method Pie Chart — UPI (26.36%), Cash (24.72%), Debit Card (24.69%), Credit Card (24.22%)
Total Quantity by Day — Line chart tracking daily quantity trends across the week
Total Quantity by Month — Monthly quantity trend line from January to December
Interactive Slicers — Filter entire dashboard by Month, Brand, and Payment Method



💡 Key Business Insights

Apple leads total sales at ₹161.6M, followed closely by OnePlus (₹153.7M) and Samsung (₹160M)
Saturday records the highest daily quantity (115M units), with a consistent downward trend toward Wednesday — suggesting weekend-driven purchasing behaviour
Payment methods are nearly equally distributed across UPI, Cash, Debit Card, and Credit Card (~25% each), indicating no single payment channel dominates
Customer ratings skew toward 5 stars (highest bar), with 4-star as the second most common — positive overall product satisfaction signal
June and July peak in monthly quantity, suggesting a mid-year demand spike possibly driven by sale season
Lucknow and Gorakhpur appear prominently on the map despite being Tier-2 cities — worth investigating as emerging markets
